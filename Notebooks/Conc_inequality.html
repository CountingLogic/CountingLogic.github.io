Concentration Inequality

Concentration inequalities dominate a lot of the literature in approximate counting. Since it is one of the topics I would like to dive into, here I try to take note of some of them.

Markov Inequality

\begin{equation} Pr(X \geq a) \leq \frac{E[X]}{a} \end{equation} Proof. \begin{align*} E[X] &= \sum x P(X = x)\\ E[X] &= \sum_{x \geq a} x P(X=x) + \sum_{x < a} x. P(X = x)\\ E[X] &\geq \sum_{x \geq a} a P(X=x)\\ E[X] &\geq a P(X\geq a)\\ \frac{E[X]}{a} &\geq P(X\geq a) \end{align*}

Chebyshev's Inequality

Given a random variable $X$, such that $X$ has a finite expected value. The standard deviation of $X$ i.e. $\sigma = \sqrt{E[(X - E[X]^{2})]}$ is finite. Then for every constant $a > 0 $: \begin{equation} Pr(|X-E[X]| \geq a) \leq \frac{\sigma^{2}}{a^2} \end{equation} Proof. \begin{equation*} Pr(|X-E[X]| \geq c) = Pr((X-E[X])^{2} \geq c^{2}) \end{equation*} Now, $|X-E[X]|$ is a finite random variable it self. Hence, using Markov Inequality, we have that: \begin{align*} Pr(|X-E[X]|^{2} \geq c^{2}) &\geq \frac{E[(X-E[X])^{2}]}{c^{2}}\\ Pr(|X-E[X]|^{2} \geq c^{2}) &\geq \frac{\sigma^{2}}{c^{2}}\\ \end{align*}
