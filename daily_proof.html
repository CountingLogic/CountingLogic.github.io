Theorem [$\epsilon$-net simple example] Let us have $m$ IID samples $S$ from an unknown distribution over the reals, if the probability of an interval $[a,b]$ is at least $\epsilon$, thenL: \begin{equation} m \geq \frac{1}{\epsilon} \ln \frac{1}{\delta} \rightarrow Pr\big[\exists. x \in S \cap [a,b] \big] \geq 1-\delta \end{equation} Proof. The probability of $x \not \in [a,b]$ is $1 - \epsilon$. The probability of sampling $m$ samples such that none of them are in $[a,b]$ is $(1-\epsilon)^{m}$. Probability that we have an example in $[a,b]$ is $1-(1-\epsilon)^{m}$. We want to lower bound $1-(1-\epsilon)^{m}$
